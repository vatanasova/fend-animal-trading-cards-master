European Polecat

Name "polecat" originates from the French phrase "poule chat" which means "the chicken cat". Name refers to the fact that polecat likes to hunt and eat chickens.

  • Scientific Name: Mustela putorius
  • Average Length: 14 to 20 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 14 years (In captivity)
  • Habitat: Western European Russia, western Belarus, western Ukraine, central and western Europe

European polecat has thick, silky yellowish-brown fur on the back and yellowish fur on the belly. It has white face and black, bandit-like mask around eyes. Fur is silver or gray-colored during the winter. It has broad head with small, rounded eyes, cylindrical, slender body, short legs and short, furry tail.
